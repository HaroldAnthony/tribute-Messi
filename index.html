Why this site exists?

July 21, 2024 by Harold Anthony

The whole purpose behind the creation of this heavily-bootstrapped webpage, and the crazy mind behind all this glazing.

SHORT STORY - I'm a college graduate, looking for an excuse to sharpen my Bootstrap skills (just effective copy-pasting).

LONG STORY - I've been crazy about football since 2011, and being an ardent follower of FC Barcelona sucks, atleast in 2024. What sucks even more is the fact that I'm not able to land a job, maybe because it is 2024. But hey, my favourite footballer just completed a three-peat of major international trophies, so it's not that bad. Well, it's not like he's aging backward, and he might retire before the potential Finalissima clash with Boy Wonder Lamine Yamal and the La Roja. But again, I'm going through a lot of emotions, and this site is where all the rambling will go on.

The Blaugrana just aren't the same without their all-time top scorer, who left amidst the club's financial trouble (and apparently, because of it!). Laporta did return as club president, and a plethora of big free agent signings did follow, but we still unearthed a lot of La Masia gems in the process. The latest one is the aforementioned Boy Wonder Lamine Yamal (that's how I'll keep calling him), but others have contributed greatly as well, including Fermín López, who was part of the UEFA EURO 2024 winning squad with Yamal, and Pau Cubarsí, who missed out. If I can include EURO-winning La Masia graduates who don't play for FC Barcelona, namely Marc Cucurella, Dani Olmo, and Alex Grimaldo (who was nowhere to be seen at the Euro, but had a fantastic season with Bayer Neverlusen), it is self-explanatory that La Masia is still the best at what it does. But the youth academy's greatest graduate is still greatly missed.

Argentina got to keep hold of their captain for longer, and coincidentally, it was after his last-ever game with FC Barcelona that he finally got to win international trophies. Maybe it was meant to be that way, starting with the 2021 Copa América, and moving on to the 2022 FIFA World Cup and the 2024 Copa América, completing the international three-peat. 2021 saw Argentina's capitán finally shrug off the criticism that he couldn't win big with the La Albiceleste, and that he always felt Catalan instead. Winning the oldest still-running continental football competition at the Maracanã, against fierce rivals Brazil, felt like destiny, although this was just the start. He would go on to be the best player of the 2022 World Cup, apart from being the winning captain, and whilst 2024's Copa América didn't go as well stat-wise, the team won gold again, and gave him perhaps his last hurrah.

"La Pulga" currently plies his trade at Inter Miami CF, which is the newest holiday destination for his Barcelona comrades Luis Suárez, Sergio Busquets and Jordi Alba. I didn't bother watching him in PSG, his next club after getting discarded from Barça, simply because I couldn't see him in in another club jersey than the accustomed blue and garnet. It must be pretty clear by now that I didn't watch him in pink as well, rather choosing to recover all that lost sleep over the years. His Sorare scores were still amazing, but he missed a handful of games, owing to fatigue and injury problems, which did prove that he was just as human like most post-prime footballers. Atleast I thought so, because watching him still reminds of yesteryear glimpses - the passing, the created chances, the occasional dribbles, and that damned walking. He's still the greatest of all time, albeit the grayed hairs.

This wannabe webpage is going to be all about THAT sportsperson, and why I've been amazed by watching his magic unfold (no pun intended).

If you're still wondering who it is.....

It's LIONEL MESSI

A 1 in 7.2 billion miracle. A genius in boots. Arguably the greatest living footballer. These are just a few terms from Prime Video's "This Is Football" documentary series, used to describe Lionel Messi.
