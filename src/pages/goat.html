From Leyenda Messi

Greatest of All Time

August 7, 2024 by Harold Anthony

MNM - Mbappé, Neymar and Messi. The deadliest trio in football was formed, atleast on paper, when the Argentine joined PSG. But how the hell did Messi decide to wear a shirt other than the striped Blaugrana?

Overview

Well, FC Barcelona's financial troubles meant they couldn't renew the contract of their biggest star, who was set to stay. It was also reported that he was open to a big paycut, because he wasn't ready to prepare an emotional final speech. But hey, football can be cruel sometimes, but atleast he was signed by a club who doubled his weekly wages. He got to play with Neymar again, who powers of persuasion paid off. He started dreaming of winning the UEFA Champions League again, which was long overdue. With possibly the best third musketeer in Kylian Mbappé, and yet another summer transfer window of blockbuster signings, the dream was realistic.

Messi in Paris was supposed to be a fairytale, but it never got to be written that way. Life after Barcelona was always bound to be hard, but it turned out to be on a higher difficulty mode. A poor first season by his standards, even though it was a good season for humanly standards, Messi just couldn't do it. In Ligue 1, he did have his moments, but on the biggest stage, the Champions League, he couldn't spew magic like he used to. This didn't go well with the fans, and Messi's deteriorating relationship the ultras didn't help either. The greatest footballer in the modern era was being booed.

The second season was much better for Leo, but continental success still eluded PSG. The club didn't sign Messi for domestic dominance - they were already good in that, for years since the Qatari takeover. They wanted the UCL, to validate a long-term sporting project with enormous investment involved. Instead, they got Messi and former on-field rival/now teammate Sergio Ramos getting all cuddly. A Saudi trip on working days was all that Messi had to do, to sever ties with the club. A suspension followed, and pretty soon, another ugly exit.

Lionel Messi's greatest achievement, whilst being a PSG player, was not at all related to the club at all. He got to win the World Cup, which surely cemented his place among the greats. His move to PSG initially meant that he could face his former club, Barcelona, at the Champions League. But as destiny would have it, in both seasons Messi played for PSG, FC Barcelona would not make it to the round of 16 - they had to worry about the UEFA Europa League instead, a new low for them. PSG didn't even make it to the same group as FCB in those 2 seasons, although Bayern Munich got that wish fulfilled, much to the dismay of the culers.

After the difficult stay in France, and winning football's greatest prize, Messi decided to put family first, and seeked a new destination that made things easy. A return to FC Barcelona, although dreamy, was completely out of reach. Al Hilal had the opportunity to recreate a club rivalry, pitting Messi against Al Nassr's Cristiano Ronaldo. But Lionel Messi chose another destination, which wasn't unexpected, but yeah, his time at Europe was done and dusted. He was finally the GOAT, football's most decorated player, and he deserved some rest.

Winning the league title in both seasons isn't really that big of an achievement for the cash-rich club. Zero Champions League titles won during Project Champions League led to the disbanding of the MNM trio. Even the wealthy Qatari ownership couldn't create an MSN regen, let alone a decent side that could actually win the Ol’ Big Ears.


Conclusion

Honestly, I've never watched a single game where Messi played for PSG, except his debut match (which was heartbreaking for the culer me). I've made sure to remain unbiased in my perspective of Messi's PSG stint, and I did watch his highlights regularly. His Opta-based scores were amazing in Sorare, which went on to prove that he was still good at what he did - play with the ball.

In an ideal world, Messi to PSG shouldn't have happened.

He was ready to stay at FC Barcelona, reportedly accepting a 50% paycut, which was huge for someone of his caliber. His family was well-settled in the Catalan city, and Messi never really wanted to leave the club of his life (except in 2020, with the whole burofax fiasco). The Blaugrana geared up for the announcement of the much-expected renewal, but overnight, things changed dramatically. The renewal was impossible, and clubs swarmed Messi like vultures, although most of them couldn't really afford him. One club, however, could play the money card, and was always the actual favourite - Manchester City. Messi was already linked with City twice, in 2016 (due to the tax evasion controversy), and in 2020, after the aforementioned burofax incident. Messi did have talks with Pep Guardiola, excited to join hands with his former manager at Barça. I too feel that Manchester City would have been a better destination, and these are my arguments -

  • The Premier League is the most attractive top-flight league, ranked first by UEFA and being the most-watched league in the world.
  • The Messi-Guardiola faction already yielded 14 trophies, including two Champions Leagues, and forming arguably the best footballing side of all time.
  • Manchester City under Guardiola was a strict team system, with no place for individual shenanigans like at PSG. Messi's job would have been much easier in Guardiola's system.
  • It would have been the perfect excuse to finally learn English. Sure, the weather and cuisine aren't the best, but a large part of Messi's global fanbase doesn't really understand what he speaks, without subtitles to save the day.

Yes, PSG made it to the UCL final in 2019/20. Neymar and Messi's reunion got a lot of people excited. Messi's second season was actually pretty good in terms of numbers. But, there's a lot of reasons that claim otherwise - the PSG transfer didn't really work out. His family had a hard time in France and he couldn't get the hero's welcome after winning the World Cup - Argentina beat France in the final, so a lot of fans were pissed back at the French capital. Besides, he didn't have the influence he enjoyed at Barça, let alone the love and the adoration.


BONUS SECTION (for the newbies)

PSG

an abbreviation for Paris Saint-Germain, a French football club based in Paris, France. Being France's most successful club, PSG competes in Ligue 1.

UEFA

stands for Union of European Football Associations, a football governing body consisting for 55 member-countries (including transcontinental countries).

FC Barcelona

Futbol Club Barcelona, a Spanish football club based in Barcelona, Catalonia. The club is known to represent Catalan culture, and competes in La Liga.

Neymar

A Brazilian footballer, known for his dribbling and flamboyance. He is often touted as the best Brazilian player of his generation, and to date, the most expensive footballer ever.

Kylian Mbappé

A French footballer, who is PSG's all-time top scorer. Winning the FIFA World Cup as a teenager, he is considered to be one of the best players in the world, currently.

UEFA Champions League

An annual club football competition contested between topmost division European clubs. It is the first-tier competition of European football, and the most prestigious one.

UEFA Europa League

It is the second-tier competition of European football, behind the UEFA Champions League. It is also an annual club football competition, contested only between eligible clubs.

Bayern Munich

a German football club based in Munich, Bavaria. Being Germany's most successful club, Bayern Munich competes in the Bundesliga, the top tier of the German football league system.

Al Nassr

A Saudi-Arabian football club based in the city of Riyadh, the club competes in Saudi Pro league, the top tier of Saudi Football. It is owned by the Public Investment Fund (state-owned).

Blaugrana

A combination of the Catalan words blau (blue) and grana (deep red) - the trademark colours in the FC Barcelona jersey, hence the team's nickname!

Barça

Another nickname for FC Barcelona, with the "ç" character from the Catalan language. The club has always represented the Catalan culture, even sporting its flag in jerseys.

Premier League

The topmost division of the English football league system, it is the most-watched sports league in the world. It is ranked at the top of Europe's top five leagues.

culers

A nickname for FC Barcelona's supporters, it is derived from the Catalan word for 'arse' (the row of bums/"culs" could be seen over the home stadium stand, during the early 1900s.)

Sergio Ramos

A Spanish footballer, known for his tackling and leadership skills. He became one of the top scorers from a defensive position, integral for Real Madrid and Spain's success in the 21st century.

Cristiano Ronaldo

A Portuguese footballer, he is widely considered as one of football's greatest players. Sharing a famous rivalry with Lionel Messi, he is also football's top goalscorer of all time.

Qatari takeover

The Qatar Sports Investments is a Qatari-government run organization, which owns the Paris Saint-Germain club. Other Gulf countries like Saudi Arabia and the UAE also own football clubs.

Al Hilal

Another Saudi-Arabian football club based in Riyadh, it is the most decorated club in Asia. It competes in the Saudi Pro League, and is also owned by the Public Investment Fund (state-owned).

Summer transfer window

A period of the year where football clubs can buy/sign players. The summer window is conducted during pre-season, held typically between June to September, but may vary between different countries/leagues.

Pep Guardiola

A Catalan football manager, who currently manages Manchester City. The only manager to win the treble twice, he is widely considered to be one of the greatest managers of all time.

Ol’ Big Ears

A nickname given to the UEFA Champions League trophy, due to its characteristic large handles that form curves, resembling the shape of ears.

Ligue 1

The topmost division of the French football league system. It is France's primary football competition and part of Europe's top five leagues.

Manchester City

a professional football club based in Manchester, England. Manchester City Football Club competes in the Premier League, and is owned by the UAE-based Abu Dhabi United Group.

Sorare

A fantasy sports game, where players are represented as digital NFT cards. The scores after each match are calculated using the Opta scoring matrix.

ultras

A type of football fans that exhibit fanatical support towards football teams. Common characteristics include chants, drums, flares, smoke bombs in creating a vocal atmosphere in favour of the supporting team.
