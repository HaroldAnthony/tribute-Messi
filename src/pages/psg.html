Messi in Paris

August 12, 2024 by Harold Anthony

MNM - Mbappe, Neymar and Messi. The deadliest trio in football was formed, atleast on paper, when the Argentine joined PSG. But how the hell did Messi decide to wear a shirt other than the striped Blaugrana?

Overview

Well, FC Barcelona's financial troubles meant they couldn't renew the contract of their biggest star, who was set to stay. It was also reported that he was open to a big paycut, because he wasn't ready to prepare an emotional final speech. But hey, football can be cruel sometimes, but atleast he was signed by a club who doubled his weekly wages. He got to play with Neymar again, who powers of persuasion paid off. He started dreaming of winning the UEFA Champions League again, which was long overdue. With possibly the best third musketeer in Kylian Mbappe, and yet another summer transfer window of blockbuster signings, the dream was realistic.

Messi in Paris was supposed to be a fairytale, but it never got to be written that way. Life after Barcelona was always bound to be hard, but it turned out to be on a higher difficulty mode. A poor first season by his standards, even though it was a good season for humanly standards, Messi just couldn't do it. In Ligue 1, he did have his moments, but on the biggest stage, the Champions League, he couldn't spew magic like he used to. This didn't go well with the fans, and Messi's deteriorating relationship the ultras didn't help either. The greatest footballer in the modern era was being booed.

The second season was much better for Leo, but continental success still eluded PSG. The club didn't sign Messi for domestic dominance - they were already good in that, for years since the Qatari takeover. They wanted the UCL, to validate a long-term sporting project with enormous investment involved. Instead, they got Messi and former on-field rival/now teammate Sergio Ramos getting all cuddly. A "Saudi trip" on working days was all that Messi had to do, to sever ties with the club. A suspension followed, and pretty soon, another ugly exit.

Lionel Messi's greatest achievement, whilst being a PSG player, was not at all related to the club at all. He got to win the World Cup, which surely cemented his place among the greats. His move to PSG initially meant that he could face his former club,"Broke Barça", at the Champions League. But as destiny would have it, in both seasons Messi played for PSG, FC Barcelona would not make it to the round of 16 - they had to worry about the UEFA Europa League instead, a new low for them. PSG didn't even make it to the same group as FCB in those 2 seasons, although Bayern Munich got that wish fulfilled, much to the dismay of the culers.

After the difficult stay in France, and winning football's greatest prize, Messi decided to put family first, and seeked a new destination that made things easy. A return to FC Barcelona, although dreamy, was completely out of reach. Al Hilal had the opportunity to recreate a club rivalry, pitting Messi against Al Nassr's Cristiano Ronaldo. But Lionel Messi chose another destination, which wasn't unexpected, but yeah, his time at Europe was done and dusted. He was finally the GOAT, football's most decorated player, and he deserved some rest.

Even the wealthy Qatari ownership couldn't create an MSN regen, let alone a decent side that could actually win the Ol’ Big Ears.


Conclusion

Honestly, I've never watched a single game where Messi played for PSG, except his debut match (which was heartbreaking for the culer me). I've made sure to remain unbiased in my perspective of Messi's PSG stint, and I did watch his highlights regularly. His OPTA-based scores were amazing in Sorare, which went on to prove that he was still good at what he did - play with the ball.

In an ideal world, Messi to PSG shouldn't have happened.

He was ready to stay at FC Barcelona, reportedly accepting a 50% paycut, which was huge for someone of his caliber. His family was well-settled in the Catalan city, and Messi never really wanted to leave the club of his life (except in 2020, with the whole burofax fiasco). The Blaugrana geared up for the announcement of the much-expected renewal, but overnight, things changed dramatically. The renewal was impossible, and clubs swarmed Messi like vultures, although most of them couldn't really afford him. Most clubs, although one club was always the actual favourite - Manchester City. Messi was already linked with City twice, in 2016 (due to the tax evasion controversy), and in 2020, after the aforementioned burofax incident. Messi did have talks with Pep Guardiola, excited to join hands with his former manager at Barça. I too feel that Manchester City would have been a better destination, and these are my arguments -

  • The Premier League is the most attractive top-flight league, ranked first by UEFA and being the most-watched league in the world.
  • The Messi-Guardiola faction already yielded 14 trophies, including two Champions Leagues, and forming arguably the best footballing side of all time.
  • Manchester City under Guardiola was a strict team system, with no place for individual shenanigans like at PSG. Messi's job would have been much easier in Guardiola's system.
  • It would have been the perfect excuse to finally learn English. Sure, the weather and cuisine aren't the best, but a large part of Messi's global fanbase doesn't really understand what he speaks, without subtitles to save the day.

Yes, PSG made it to the UCL final in 2019/20. Neymar and Messi's reunion got a lot of people excited. Messi's second season was actually pretty good in terms of numbers. But, there's a lot of reasons that claim otherwise - the PSG transfer didn't really work out. His family had a hard time in France and he couldn't get the hero's welcome after winning the World Cup - Argentina beat France in the final, so a lot of fans were pissed back at the French capital. Besides, he didn't have the influence he enjoyed at Barça, let alone the love and the adoration.


BONUS SECTION (for the newbies)

If you reached this section after reading the whole damn thing, I have to tip my hat to your dedication. However, if you're new to football, there might be certain words you couldn't understand, and maybe you're too lazy to Google them.

Well, I hear you, and this section would help you, for now. All the very best and, HAPPY EXPLORING!

Copa América

The top football tournament contested among the national teams from South America. However, teams from North America and Asia have often been invited to take part.

UEFA EURO

The primary football tournament contested between UEFA member nations. It includes all European countries, as well as Turkey, Azerbaijan, Georgia, Kazakhstan, Cyprus, Armenia and Israel.

Finalissima

A one-off match contested between the UEFO EURO winner and the Copa América winner. It was only held in 1985 and 1993, before being relaunched in 2022.

La Albiceleste

A nickname for the Argentina national football team, which roughly represents the national flag's colours, using the Spanish words for white and sky blue.

La Roja

Roughly translated as "The Red One" from Spanish, it is a nickname for the Spain national football team, owing to the red jerseys worn by its players.

Bayer Neverlusen

A nickname for Bayer Leverkusen, a German football club, referring to their historic unbeaten run in the 2023-24 Bundesliga, the German top-flight league competition.

Blaugrana

A combination of the Catalan words blau (blue) and grana (deep red) - the trademark colours in the FC Barcelona jersey, hence the team's nickname!

Barça

Another nickname for FC Barcelona, with the "ç" character from the Catalan language. The club has always represented the Catalan culture, even sporting its flag in jerseys.

blue and garnet

Another nickname denoting the primary colours of the FC Barcelona home jersey, although it's in English, so it must be easy for the newbies. These colours are often represented as trademark stripes in the jersey.

La Masia

The youth academy of FC Barcelona, which is known for its distinct philosophy, and producing many world-class players, often instrumental in the success of the senior FC Barcelona side.

La Pulga

A Spanish term, which translates to "The Flea", a childhood nickname for Lionel Messi, owing to his small stature as a kid (due to a growth hormone deficiency). I'd still call him the GOAT though.

Sorare

A fantasy sports game, where players are represented as digital NFT cards. The scores after each match are calculated using the Opta scoring matrix.
